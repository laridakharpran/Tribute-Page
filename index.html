About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the 11th President of India, was a visionary scientist, a humble leader, and an inspiring teacher. Known for spearheading India's space and missile programs, he left a legacy of wisdom, simplicity, and innovation.

Timeline of Kalam's Life

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College.
  • 1960: Earned a degree in Aeronautical Engineering.
  • 1980: Led the successful launch of SLV-III.
  • 1998: Chief project coordinator for Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the President of India.
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
